Imagine a digital wallet containing all of your identity documents which are just as authentic and secure as the original paper copies. To prove your identity, you would just provide limited access to this wallet. No need to certify copies or scan paper documents. This is just one of the many potential uses of Blockchain in government.


Blockchain is a relatively new technology that emerged from the creation of Bitcoin. Now, you may be thinking, Bitcoin doesn’t really relate to government and whilst that may be mostly true (for now), the applications of Blockchain extend well beyond cryptocurrencies.


Blockchain is a decentralised digital ledger, where transactions are recorded chronologically and publically. What makes Blockchain so special is that it’s an incorruptible ledger that can record everything of value, making it perfect to ensure the authenticity of data. Due to Blockchain’s decentralised form, there is significantly improved security over the traditional data storage in centralised servers.


The Government of Illinois, have begun the ‘Blockchain transformation’ with a specific focus on identity registries, compliance and reporting ledgers and benefits and entitlement ledgers. This focus is due to the relative ease of implementation as Blockchain has been proven for these use cases.
It’s also worth noting that Blockchain does have a few drawbacks. Due to its decentralised system, the resulting performance is slow which can affect transaction times. It also has limited storage capacity and difficulties with legacy integration. This could affect more complex ledgers with large file sizes such as medical records.


Regardless of these limitations , Blockchain can provide real benefits to industries such as health, finance, enforcement, tax and more. As Blockchain continues to mature, government should start to think about how it can be used to improve services.
